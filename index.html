Introduction

Networks are the backbone of modern communication. Whether in homes, offices, or across the globe, networking enables devices to share data, access the internet, and connect users efficiently. In this portfolio, you’ll learn about network types, topologies, essential tools, and how cabling and testing ensure proper network performance.

Types and Topologies of Network

Types

LAN (Local Area Network):
A LAN connects computers and devices within a small, localized area such as an office, school, or home. It typically uses Ethernet or Wi-Fi for high-speed data sharing.
WAN (Wide Area Network):
A WAN connects multiple LANs across large geographic regions—cities, countries, or even continents. The Internet is the largest WAN in existence.
MAN (Metropolitan Area Network):
A MAN covers a larger area than a LAN but smaller than a WAN, such as a city or campus. It’s often used by organizations or municipalities.
PAN (Personal Area Network):
A PAN connects personal devices like smartphones, tablets, and laptops over short distances, often using Bluetooth or USB.

Topologies

Bus Topology:
All devices share a single communication line. It’s simple but can cause collisions if too many devices send data at once.
Star Topology:
Each device connects to a central hub or switch. This design is common in LANs and allows easy troubleshooting if one device fails.
Ring Topology:
Devices are connected in a circular path. Data travels in one direction around the ring until it reaches its destination.
Mesh Topology:
Each device connects directly to others, creating multiple pathways for data. This provides high redundancy and reliability.
Hybrid Topology:
A combination of two or more topologies, often used in large, complex networks to increase efficiency and fault tolerance.

Networking Technologies

Ethernet

The most widely used wired LAN technology that supports speeds from 10 Mbps to 100 Gbps.

Wi-Fi (Wireless Fidelity)

Enables devices to connect without cables using radio signals, ideal for mobility and convenience.

Bluetooth

Short-range wireless technology for connecting personal devices like headsets, keyboards, and phones.

VPN (Virtual Private Network)

Creates a secure, encrypted tunnel between a user and a remote network, ensuring privacy and data protection.

5G / Cellular Networks

High-speed mobile technology enabling fast data transfer for mobile devices and IoT systems.

Networking Tools

Crimping Tool

Used to attach RJ45 connectors to Ethernet cables.

Multimeter

Measures voltage, resistance, and current in electrical circuits, useful for diagnosing power issues.

Network Analyzer Software

Monitors, captures, and analyzes data packets to identify network issues or optimize performance.

Cable Tester

Verifies the connectivity and signal integrity of cables to detect faults or miswiring.

Punch-Down Tool

Used to connect wires into patch panels or network jacks securely.

Cabling

Cable Type Maximum Distance Typical Use
Twisted Pair (Cat5e, Cat6) Up to 100 meters Common Ethernet LAN connections
Coaxial Cable Up to 500 meters Cable Internet, CCTV, and legacy networks
Fiber Optic Cable Several kilometers High-speed data backbones and long-distance connections

Cable Testing

Before deploying any network cable, technicians must perform proper testing to ensure signal quality and connectivity.
Cable testing checks for:

  • Wire continuity
  • Correct pin configuration
  • Signal strength and interference
  • Proper termination
